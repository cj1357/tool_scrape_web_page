Why These Agents Keep Getting Smarter

Traditional RPA is like a train on tracks: once the route is set, it never swerves. Agentic AI is the drone that spots a storm ahead, recalculates, and still delivers the package on time.

The difference is continuous learning. While RPA follows the same fixed rules until a developer rewrites them, Make agents use reinforcement learning and human-in-the-loop feedback to improve after every single run.

This is the heart of agentic AI vs RPA. RPA bots freeze when they meet data they have not seen before; Make agents review the new pattern, check the reward signal, and update their next move.

Imagine a support ticket that arrives with slang the bot has never met. An RPA script would mark it “error” and wait. A Make agent scores its own answer against the customer’s emoji reaction, keeps the good parts, and tries a friendlier tone next time.
