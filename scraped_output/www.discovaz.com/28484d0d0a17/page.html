Use ACES and PIES Data to Get Found

That tiny $50 post can only snowball if shoppers can actually find the part. Google, Amazon, and even your own site search all lean on two quiet helpers: ACES and PIES vehicle fitment data. When those spreadsheets are clean, the part shows up for every exact match, returns drop, and the algorithm starts pushing you higher—exactly the momentum you need.

Think of ACES as the backstage pass that tells every system, "This rotor fits a 2012-18 Ford Focus with 2.0 engine and rear drums." PIES adds price, weight, and photos. Together they turn a blurry listing into a neon sign search engines trust. The payoff? Accurate listings cut costly returns and lift visibility so one post keeps earning year after year.

Three quick checks you can do today to see if your fitment data is ACES-ready:

  • Open the free ACES template and confirm every part has a unique part number, brand ID, and at least one vehicle configuration
  • Scan for blank Year-Make-Model columns; fill any gaps using your supplier catalog or a VIN decoder
  • Run the file through the ACES validator tool; fix red-flagged rows before uploading to Google Merchant Center

Once your ACES and PIES files are tidy, you are primed for the next lever: speed. A clean feed loads faster in search results and ads, and shoppers hate waiting. Let’s make sure your pages open before their patience closes.

Let Customers Sell for You With UGC Photos

Mindy in Milwaukee snapped a quick pic of her ten-year-old handing her the new floormats for their Tahoe. She tagged the shop and added #WeekendWrenching. By Monday the shop had eight more orders for that exact set. The secret? User-generated content for auto parts turns happy buyers into unpaid sales reps, and every UGC photo quietly adds a 3.2% conversion lift to the snowball you already started with that $50 post.

You do not need models, lights, or fancy cameras. You just need permission, a phone, and a tiny bit of manners. One buyer’s lift-kit selfie can live on the product page for years, doing the heavy lifting while you sip coffee. Below is the 10-minute flow so you can grab, approve, and publish a customer photo before your next sip gets cold.

  1. Ask: Drop a quick DM or email right after delivery. Say, “Mind if we share your awesome pic? We’ll tag you and send a 10% coupon for your next order.”
  2. Get written yes: A thumbs-up emoji counts. Screenshot it.
  3. Grab rights: Add a note in your Shopify backend or simple spreadsheet: name, date, platform, permission.
  4. Resize: Crop square for Instagram, 4:3 for product page, 16:9 for Facebook—30 seconds in any free app.
  5. Upload: Drop the photo into the gallery slot on the part’s page. Add alt-text with the part number plus “installed by customer.”
  6. Tag: Paste customer’s social handle in the caption and add #InstalledByYou.
  7. Pin: Stick the post to the top of your feed for 48 hours so new eyes see it.
  8. Track: Note the date you published; watch the next seven days for that sweet 3.2% conversion lift.

A polite heads-up keeps everyone happy. Always tag the original poster and never edit their image beyond a gentle crop. If they say no, thank them anyway and move on. When they say yes, the photo becomes evergreen proof that real people bolt your parts on real rides.
